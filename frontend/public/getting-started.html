Step 8b

Specify child relationship types

Not all parent-child bonds are the same. OurFamRoots supports biological, adoptive, step, and foster relationships.

  • 1
    Click a person, then "Add child"
    When adding a child to an existing parent, the form lets you specify the relationship type
  • 2
    Choose the parentage type
    Biological — birth parent (solid line)
    Adoptive — legally adopted (long dash)
    Step — through marriage (short dash)
    Foster — foster care (dash-dot)
  • 3
    Visual line styles tell the story
    Each type uses a distinct line pattern on the canvas so you can instantly see the relationship at a glance
  • 4
    Labels appear on the edge
    Non-biological edges show a small label ("adopted", "step", "foster") on the connecting line
💡
You can change a child's parentage type later by clicking the child, opening the panel, and editing their relationship to the parent.

Record the full picture of family relationships — marriages, partnerships, and divorces.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Members not showing in tree?

⚠️
Problem: You added people but some (or all) of them are missing from the canvas — the tree looks incomplete or blank.
  • 1
    Open the Layouts panel
    Click the Layouts button in the canvas top bar (top-right area). This opens the layout & view-control panel.
  • 2
    Click "Expand all branches"
    Look for the ⊞ Expand all branches icon or button. This forces every collapsed node and hidden sub-branch to become visible on the canvas.
  • 3
    Select "Vertical ↕"
    Click the ↕ Vertical layout icon. This resets the tree to a clean multi-marriage-aware top-down arrangement so all members are laid out in view.
💡
Still not seeing everyone? Scroll/zoom out on the canvas — some nodes may be off-screen to the right or below. Press Ctrl+Space to search for a specific person by name and jump directly to them.

💡 Tips & Tricks

Power moves with Ctrl

Hold Ctrl on the canvas to unlock drag-and-drop control — without accidentally opening someone's profile.

  • 1
    Drag any person node
    Hold Ctrl + press and hold the left mouse button on a person card, then drag it to a new position. Release to drop.
  • 2
    Multi-select members
    Hold Ctrl and click each person node you want. All clicked nodes are selected at once — useful for moving a whole generation together.
  • 3
    Drag a union ring
    Hold Ctrl + press and hold on the dashed union ring (💑) between two spouses, then drag to reposition the coupling node independently.
💡
Why Ctrl? A plain click on any node opens its detail panel. Holding Ctrl switches the canvas into drag/select mode so you can rearrange without accidentally opening profiles.

💡 Tips & Tricks

More canvas tips to know

Master these everyday interactions to work faster and smarter.

  • 4
    Zoom in & out
    Roll the scroll wheel (mouse) or use a two-finger pinch (trackpad) anywhere on the canvas to zoom.
  • 5
    Pan the canvas
    Click and drag on any empty area of the canvas (not on a node) to scroll in any direction.

    Click any node (without Ctrl) to open the side panel — shows relatives, photo, and edit options.
